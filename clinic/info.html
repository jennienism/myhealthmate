WHAT IS MENTAL HEALTH?

Mental health includes our emotional, psychological, and social well-being. It affects how we think, feel, and act, and helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices.

Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood. Over the course of your life, if you experience mental health problems, your thinking, mood, and behavior could be affected.

HEALTH TIPS

@careyourhealth

Top 5 Health Tips

  • Stay hydrated by drinking at least 8 glasses of water daily.
  • Engage in regular physical activities such as walking or yoga.
  • Maintain a balanced diet with plenty of fruits and vegetables.
  • Get adequate sleep every night for better mental and physical health.
  • Practice mindfulness and manage stress effectively.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Why is hydration important?

Hydration is essential for maintaining body temperature, joint lubrication, and overall health.

2. How often should I exercise?

It is recommended to engage in moderate exercise at least 150 minutes per week.

3. What are the benefits of a balanced diet?

A balanced diet provides essential nutrients, boosts energy, and supports overall well-being.

Calories & Mental Health

"A healthy mind resides in a healthy body."

Understanding Calories

Calories fuel our body for daily activities. Whether you're a student or staff, maintaining the right balance is essential.

  • Students: Need 2,000–2,500 calories daily depending on activity level, age, and gender.
  • Staff: Require 1,800–2,200 calories, influenced by activity level and age.

Calories and Mental Health

Your brain uses about 20% of your daily calories. Proper nutrition supports mental clarity and emotional balance.

  • Low energy intake can reduce cognitive performance.
  • Unhealthy diets may increase stress and anxiety.
  • Key nutrients support brain health and mood stability.

The Importance of Mental Health

Mental health enables you to handle life's challenges, work productively, and build meaningful relationships.

  • Students: Stay focused and manage stress effectively.
  • Staff: Make better decisions and enhance productivity.
  • Everyone: Enjoy life and build resilience.
